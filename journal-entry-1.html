Democritus & Atomism

"By convention sweet is sweet, bitter is bitter, hot is hot, cold is cold, color is color; but in truth there are only atoms and the void." — Democritus

Everything started with Democritus. He was an ancient Greek philosopher and a student of Leucippus (actually, he's credited as the founder of Atomism—the belief that the universe is made of these fundamental, indivisible little things called atoms. Later on, his student helped develop the idea even more).

Back in 400 B.C., Democritus called these tiny, "uncuttable" things "atomos" (spoiler: he was wrong).

He also claimed that everything—EVERYTHING—is made of atoms, and that atoms are indestructible. Meaning? They literally cannot be destroyed. He said they're always moving and will never stop—basically the hyper little things of the universe. And of course, he believed there are infinite numbers of atoms, in infinite kinds, all with different shapes and sizes (spoiler: also VERY wrong).
